Kimi Räikkönen

The McLaren years

Kimi Räikkönen performs his greatest win at 2005 Japanese GP

Here's a time line of Kimi's McLaren years:

  • 2002 - Australian GP: Finishes 3rd in his first race with McLaren after surviving a huge start crash
  • 2002 - French GP: First chance for victory, but because of an oil spill on track Schumacher inherits the win and secure his 5th world title
  • 2002 - Finishes the season 6th with 24 points and 4 podiums
  • 2003 - Malaysian GP: First win makes him the 2nd youngest winner at the time
  • 2003 - European GP: After qualifying for pole for the 1st time and leading the race for 25 laps an engine failure ends his race
  • 2003 - After fighting for the championship all year long Kimi achieves second place with 91 points, 1 victory and 10 podiums in total
  • 2004 - Belgian GP: After many engine failures during the year Kimi scores second win in Spa-Franchorchamp
  • 2004 - Unsuccessful year: seventh place in championship after 1 victory, 4 podiums and 8 retirements out of 18 races
  • 2005 - New teammate: Juan Pablo Montoya joins McLaren
  • 2005 - USA GP: DNS for all Michelin-tyre cars. Boycott in F1!
  • 2005 - Japanese GP: Kimi wins the race from 17th grid position by overtaking Fisichella in the last lap. Best victory ever!
  • 2005 - Fight for championship: 2nd place behind Alonso with 112 points, 7 wins and 12 podiums
  • 2006 - Unsuccesful year again: fifth place in championship with 65 points, 6 podiums and no win
